Wella Color Perfect 7 – Usage Guidelines

CONTENT OF THE PACKAGE:

1 stable dye cream

1 lightener

1 oil based serum for the 1st day

1 color activator for the 15th day

1 oil based serum for the 30th day

1 pair of gloves

Each time you color your hair, test the dye on a section of hair to determine the optimal dyeing time and result. Chemical perming, hair straighteners, previous coloring and the sun can all affect your results and timing. You will need scissors, scotch and a timer.

1. Cut a 1 cm wide strand of hair from the root in an inconspicuous area (anywhere but NOT along the parting, temples or hairline) and tie off with the scotch, leaving the root tip exposed.

2. Cover the root tip completely with the mixture left after the allergy test. Start the timer.

3. In 35 minutes (45 minutes if you use 12/0, 12/1, 12/11 and 12/81 shades) check the strand. If you did not get your desired color, cover the strand again with the mixture, leaving it another 5 or 10 minutes.

Remember the time it takes to get your desired color.

PREPARATION

1. Put on the gloves.

2. Open the bottle of lightener (2)

3. Open the tube of stable dye cream (1) and pour the dye in the bottle of the lightener (2)

4. Close the bottle and shake until getting a homogeneous mixture.

Note: do not close the bottle again after mixture and do not store it in a closed container as it may explode.

APPLYING TO HAIR
AS A NEW COLOR IF YOU HAVE NEVER DYED YOUR HAIR OR NEVER DYED YOUR HAIR OR THE LAST TIME DYED MORE THAN THREE MONTHS AGO

Apply the dye to the entire length of your hair.

Apply the mixture immediately to dry and unwashed hair starting from the roots. You may divide your hair into sections to apply the dye more evenly.

SPREAD THROUGH THE ENTIRE LENGTH OF THE HAIR, from roots to ends for full saturation. Leave it on for 40 minutes.

When using shades 12/0, 12/1, 12/11 and 12/81, apply first to the entire length and ends, avoiding the roots. Leave it on for 20 minutes. Then spread it through the entire length of your hair, applying also to roots. Leave it on for another 30 minutes.

IF YOU DYE YOUR HAIR FREQUENTLY (ONCE IN 1 TO 3 MONTHS) WITH SIMILAR SHADES, THEN DYE THE ROOTS FIRST FOR AN EVEN DYEING FROM ROOT TO TIPS

Divide the dry and unwashed hair to 2cm strands with the tip of the applicator. Apply the mixture only to newly grown areas until fully absorbed, working from forehead to nape. You may divide your hair into sections to apply the dye more evenly. The mixture may have a different color, which does NOT represent the final result of the dyeing.

KEEP THE DYE ON YOUR HAIR: WAIT

The stylists are aware that it takes more time to dye newly grown hair. Moreover, focusing on newly grown hair helps protect the ends from further damage.

Keep the mixture on the newly grown part of the roots for 30 minutes. When using shades 12/0, 12/1, 12/11 and 12/81, leave on for 40 minutes.

APPLY TO THE REST OF THE HAIR

Apply the remained mixture to the rest of your hair. Apply from roots to ends for full saturation. Leave on for another 10 minutes.

IMPROVEMENT OF RESULTS
WASHING THE HAIR AND APPLYING CONDITIONER

1. After the time is up, wash your hair until the water runs clear (shampoo is not recommended).

2. Apply the first pack of oil based serum on the day of dyeing.

3. Leave on for 2 minutes and wash again.

In 15 days

1. Apply the 15th day color activator to the wet hair.

2. Leave on for 10 minutes. Wash and style your hair as usual.

In 30 days

1. Apply the second pack of oil based serum.

2. Leave on for 2 minutes. Wash and style your hair as usual.

INSTRUCTION FOR USE

Perform an allergy alert test 48 hours before dyeing the hair, even though you have previously used hair color products.

Open the color cream box. Use the sharp end of the cap to pierce the metal foil of the tube without squeezing it and keeping away from the face.
Open the developer.
Prepare a cotton swab, a small plastic container and a small plastic spoon.
Mix small equal amounts of color cream and developer in a plastic container. In case of colors 12/0 and 12/1, use the ratio of 1-2 when mixing the color cream with the developer.
Close firmly the color cream and developer.
Apply the mixture to an area of 1cm² on your inner elbow by the cotton pad. Leave it to become dry. Avoid wetting the test area for 48 hours. To prevent wetting bind the test area with waterproof bandage and remove the bandage immediately after contact with water.
Control the test area during 48 hours. In the absence of symptoms of an allergic reaction you may proceed with hair dyeing.
This test is an important precaution. However, absence of an allergic reaction is not a guarantee that you will not experience it during the next hair dyeing. To avoid random, inaccurate results of the test, we recommend consulting an allergist.
Notice: Rash, redness, burning or itching may be symptoms of an allergic reaction. In such cases dyeing hair with that product is not recommended.

HOW OT APPLY THE DYE WHEN DYEING THE HAIR FOR THE FIRST TIME

Step 1
Divide the hair into separate equal strands by a comb.
STEP 2
Apply the coloring mixture starting from the roots and the gray areas of the hair. Using a wide tooth comb distribute the coloring mixture to the hair evenly.
STEP 3
Soaking time is 30-35 minutes. For more intense/optimum result, for color shades, leave the mixture on the hair for up to 40 minutes. Attention: in the case of colors 12/0 and 12/1, leave the mixture on the hair for up to 50 minutes.
STEP 4
After soaking time is over lather the coloring mixture with a small amount of hot water. Lightly massage the scalp, then wash your hair thoroughly until the water runs clear.

OUR HISTORY

WELLA HERITAGE

HISTORY OF EXPLORATION AND INSPIRATION

1880

Before he changed the world of hairdressing forever, Franz Stroher traveled through France, Holland, Germany and Switzerland as an apprentice hairstylist. While on his journey, he recognized the strong connection between "de rigueur" hair and fashion trends. By emphasizing the importance of understanding and innovating for the fashions of the day, and the woman who weras them, Stroher lay the enduring foundation for Wela's philosophy.
